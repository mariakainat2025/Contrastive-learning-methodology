Tactic Name: Persistence
ATT&CK ID: TA0003

Objective
The adversary is trying to maintain their foothold by retaining access across system restarts, credential changes, and other interruptions that might otherwise sever their presence.

Tactical Intent
Adversaries use this tactic to ensure continued access after Initial Access and Execution, reducing the need to re-compromise the target. Persistence enables long-term operations supporting Privilege Escalation, Lateral Movement, and Command and Control.

Behavior Characteristics
Persistence is characterized by adversaries modifying system state to survive disruptions through three behavioral families:

Modification of startup and autorun configurations to ensure malicious code executes after restarts or logon events.
Creation or manipulation of accounts and authentication mechanisms to maintain access independent of the initial compromise vector.
Implantation or modification of system processes, services, and software components to repeatedly execute malicious payloads.
These behaviors indicate attempts to maintain long-term unauthorized access within the target environment.

Observable Indicators

Configuration change to startup, boot, or logon mechanism followed by new execution event.
Account or authentication artifact created or modified outside normal administrative activity.
System process, service, or scheduled mechanism modified with unfamiliar binary or script.
Legitimate software binary or component modified followed by unexpected execution event.
New persistent connection or access channel established following system configuration change.

Log Sources
Detection opportunities exist in process creation and service logs capturing autostart execution, registry and file modification logs identifying changes to startup mechanisms, account management logs monitoring unauthorized account creation or credential changes, and authentication logs detecting modifications to access control mechanisms.
