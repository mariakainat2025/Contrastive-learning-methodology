======================================================================
  MITRE ATT&CK — TACTIC: TA0040
======================================================================
  ID         : TA0040
  NAME       : Impact

  OBJECTIVE  : The adversary is trying to interfere with, damage, or alter systems and data.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
  TECHNIQUES USE
----------------------------------------------------------------------

  Impact consists of adversary techniques aimed at disrupting system
  availability or degrading data integrity by manipulating operational
  processes. These actions may include deletion, corruption, or
  modification of critical data.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
  OUTCOME / TACTIC LINKS
----------------------------------------------------------------------

  Systems may continue to function normally on the surface while
  internal data or processes have been altered. These techniques are
  often used to achieve final objectives or obscure prior compromise.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
  LINUX DETECTION STRATEGIES
----------------------------------------------------------------------

  Detection of password or account changes using passwd, userdel, or
  chage following suspicious remote access or execution from
  low-privilege contexts.

  Identification of large-scale file destruction using rm -rf, shred,
  dd, or wiping tools, including mass unlink activity and deletion of
  system or user data.

  Detection of encryption-based disruption using tools like openssl,
  gpg, or aescrypt operating recursively across directories, often
  combined with data overwrite and ransom artifacts.

  Monitoring unauthorized modification of logs, databases, or
  configuration files via auditd and syslog correlation with shell
  behavior that alters HISTFILE or related artifacts.

  Detection of web content tampering affecting Apache, Nginx, or other
  web services through malicious script execution.

  Detection of destructive disk operations using dd, shred, or wipe
  targeting physical or virtual block devices, including bootloader or
  partition modifications.

  Monitoring for abnormal mail system activity such as excessive
  delivery volume or repeated attachment writes to mail directories.

  Detection of system instability caused by malicious activity, such as
  repeated service crashes, kernel panics, or forced restarts of
  critical services like nginx, httpd, or sshd.

  Monitoring outbound connections to cryptocurrency mining infrastructure
  or financial systems linked to unauthorized processes or scripts.

  Detection of firmware or memory manipulation attempts via direct
  access to /dev/mem or /sys/firmware and use of flashing utilities.

  Detection of deletion or modification of recovery-related system files
  such as /etc/fstab backups, EFI partitions, or rescue boot targets,
  sometimes combined with attribute locking via chattr.

  High-volume network flooding using tools like hping3 or nping across
  multiple targets or ports.

  Detection of abnormal resource consumption linked to unauthorized
  processes, often associated with mining malware or persistence scripts.

  Detection of service termination actions (systemctl stop, service
  stop) against critical services followed by destructive operations on
  system or application data.

  Detection of shutdown or reboot commands executed outside expected
  administrative maintenance windows.
