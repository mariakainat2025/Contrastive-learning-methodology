======================================================================
  MITRE ATT&CK — TACTIC: TA0011
======================================================================
  ID         : TA0011
  NAME       : Command and Control

  OBJECTIVE  : The adversary is attempting to communicate with compromised systems in order to maintain control.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
  TECHNIQUES USE
----------------------------------------------------------------------

  Command and Control consists of techniques used by adversaries to
  maintain communication with systems they control inside a victim
  network. Attackers often attempt to blend malicious traffic with
  normal network activity to avoid detection. C2 channels can vary
  widely in stealth depending on network topology and defensive
  controls.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
  LINUX DETECTION STRATEGIES
----------------------------------------------------------------------

  Detection of suspicious curl, wget, or custom socket traffic over DNS,
  HTTPS, or IRC-like protocols exhibiting beaconing behavior or
  irregular periodic communication.

  Monitoring file writes to removable mount points (/media, /run/media)
  followed by execution or reuse of transferred artifacts on different
  hosts.

  Detection of staged payload downloads via curl/wget into /tmp or
  /var/tmp followed by execution, correlated with IDS/IPS alerts or
  unexpected content type responses.

  Outbound data encoded using Base64, gzip, or hex prior to transmission
  through curl, wget, or raw socket connections, typically from
  non-standard processes.

  Identification of unknown or custom processes generating high-volume
  HTTP traffic with unusually low response payloads.

  DNS resolver logs and audit records showing repeated failed queries or
  access to high-entropy or algorithmically generated domains, correlated
  with interpreter-based process chains (bash, Python, etc.).

  TLS/SSL connections initiated by curl, wget, python, socat, or custom
  binaries targeting unusual external endpoints, with persistent encrypted
  sessions and connect() syscall activity.

  Detection of covert communication channels using ICMP, DNS tunneling,
  or unconventional ports originating from non-network-oriented processes.

  Detection of firewall or proxy rule manipulation via iptables or
  nftables intended to evade monitoring or filtering systems.

  Shell tools like curl, wget, or scp used to retrieve external payloads
  followed by execution of downloaded artifacts.

  Multi-step execution chains where downloaded content is staged to disk
  or memory and then triggers secondary outbound communication.

  ICMP or raw socket traffic generated by user-space tools such as bash,
  Python, netcat, hping3, or scapy without normal administrative context.

  Detection of services binding to non-standard ports or initiating
  connections with incorrect protocol/port mappings.

  SSH, socat, or custom binaries used for tunneling, reverse shells, or
  port forwarding, often encapsulating protocols like RDP or SMB over
  encrypted channels.

  Detection of misuse of user-space tools for tunneling, reverse shells,
  or inter-host communication with atypical external destinations.

  RAT-like behavior involving systemd-based persistence and persistent
  external communication, sometimes spawning interactive shells.

  Detection of port-knocking activity followed by firewall changes or
  dynamically enabled listening services.

  Detection of HTTPS-based C2 traffic targeting services like Discord,
  Slack, Dropbox, or cloud APIs in unusual operational contexts.
