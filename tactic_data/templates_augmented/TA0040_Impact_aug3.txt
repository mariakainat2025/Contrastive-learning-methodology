======================================================================
  MITRE ATT&CK — TACTIC: TA0040
======================================================================
  ID         : TA0040
  NAME       : Impact

  OBJECTIVE  : The adversary is attempting to disrupt, damage, or manipulate system and data integrity.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
  TECHNIQUES USE
----------------------------------------------------------------------

  Impact consists of techniques used by adversaries to disrupt system
  availability or compromise integrity through manipulation of business
  and operational workflows. This may involve deletion, alteration, or
  corruption of data and system state.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
  OUTCOME / TACTIC LINKS
----------------------------------------------------------------------

  Systems may appear to operate normally while internal processes or
  datasets have been modified. These techniques are commonly used to
  achieve end goals or mask prior intrusion activity.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
  LINUX DETECTION STRATEGIES
----------------------------------------------------------------------

  Detection of account changes or password modifications using passwd,
  userdel, or chage following suspicious shell sessions or remote
  execution from non-privileged users.

  Detection of mass file deletion or destruction using rm -rf, shred,
  dd, or wipe utilities, including large-scale unlink operations and
  removal of system data.

  Detection of ransomware-style encryption using openssl, gpg, or
  aescrypt applied recursively to directories, often paired with data
  overwriting and ransom artifact creation.

  Monitoring unauthorized changes to logs, databases, or configuration
  files via auditd/syslog correlation, especially when HISTFILE or
  logging artifacts are modified.

  Detection of web defacement activity targeting Apache, Nginx, or
  similar services through malicious script execution.

  Detection of destructive disk access using dd, shred, or wipe
  targeting raw devices such as /dev/sda or /dev/nvme0n1, including
  boot or partition modifications.

  Monitoring abnormal mail system behavior, including excessive message
  volumes or repeated writes to mail storage directories.

  Detection of system disruption events such as repeated crashes,
  kernel panics, or forced restarts of services like nginx, httpd, or
  sshd.

  Detection of unauthorized outbound connections to cryptocurrency
  mining pools or financial systems tied to malicious scripts or
  processes.

  Detection of direct firmware or memory access through /dev/mem or
  /sys/firmware and use of flashing utilities.

  Detection of tampering with recovery-critical files such as fstab,
  rescue targets, or EFI boot partitions, often combined with attribute
  locking via chattr.

  Detection of network flooding using tools like hping3 or nping across
  multiple ports, hosts, or services.

  Detection of abnormal resource usage tied to unauthorized processes,
  often associated with mining malware or persistence mechanisms.

  Detection of service shutdown operations (systemctl stop, service stop)
  against critical infrastructure followed by destructive file activity.

  Detection of shutdown, reboot, or poweroff commands executed outside
  approved maintenance or administrative contexts.
